# Corporate income tax: filing obligations and deadlines (synthetic demo doc)

**Last verified:** 2026-06-29. Source: the tax authority, "Corporate Income Tax"
filing guides (https://www.iras.gov.sg/taxes/corporate-income-tax). Demo document
for the Corporate Income Tax workspace; facts paraphrased, not reproduced verbatim.

## Facts
- **Two filings each Year of Assessment (YA):** an Estimated Chargeable Income
  (ECI) filing, then the annual Corporate Income Tax Return.
- **ECI** is normally due within **3 months of the company's financial year
  end**, unless the waiver conditions are met (annual revenue of $5 million or
  below and ECI is nil).
- **The income tax return** (Form C-S, Form C-S (Lite), or Form C) is due by
  **30 November** of the YA, e-filed at mytax.iras.gov.sg (e-filing is
  compulsory).
- **Form choice by annual revenue:** Form C-S (Lite) if revenue is **$200,000 or
  below**; Form C-S if revenue is **$5 million or below** (and other conditions
  are met); otherwise **Form C**, which requires financial statements and a tax
  computation to be submitted.
- **Late or non-filing** can lead to penalties, an estimated Notice of
  Assessment, summons, and enforcement. A dormant or loss-making company must
  still file unless a waiver applies.

## Summary (own words)
A company files twice per YA: an early estimate (ECI) within three months of its
financial year end, then the actual return by 30 November. Which return form it
uses depends on revenue, smaller companies get the simpler C-S or C-S (Lite).
Most filing casework is about the form and threshold choice and chasing late
filers, since the deadline itself is fixed.

## Officer notes
- Derive the ECI due date from the company's financial year end; the return is
  always due 30 November regardless of year end.
- Confirm the revenue band before advising Form C-S vs C-S (Lite) vs C.
- Dormant and loss-making companies still have to file unless granted a waiver.
