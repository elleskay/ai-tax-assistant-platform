# Corporate income tax: rates and exemptions (synthetic demo doc)

**Last verified:** 2026-06-28. Source: the tax authority, "Corporate Income Tax
rates and exemption schemes" (https://www.iras.gov.sg). Demo document for the
Corporate workspace.

## Facts
- **Corporate income tax rate: 17%** (flat) on chargeable income.
- **Start-Up Tax Exemption (SUTE)**: for qualifying new companies, partial
  exemption on the first slices of normal chargeable income for the first 3 YAs.
- **Partial Tax Exemption (PTE)**: available to most other companies on the first
  slices of normal chargeable income.
- Chargeable income is income after deductible expenses, capital allowances, and
  approved donations.

## Summary (own words)
Companies are taxed at a flat 17% on chargeable income, but exemption schemes
(start-up and partial) reduce the effective rate on the first portion of income.
Determining chargeable income, and which exemption applies, is where most
corporate casework sits, because the rate itself is simple.

## Officer notes
- Confirm whether the company qualifies for SUTE (new, qualifying) or PTE before
  quoting an effective rate.
- Complex corporate cases (restructuring, group relief, transfer pricing) need
  premium-model reasoning and careful citation; this workspace defaults to a
  premium model for that reason.
