# Corporate income tax: deductions, allowances, and reliefs (synthetic demo doc)

**Last verified:** 2026-06-29. Source: the tax authority, "Corporate Income Tax"
income & deductions guides (https://www.iras.gov.sg/taxes/corporate-income-tax).
Demo document for the Corporate Income Tax workspace; facts paraphrased.

## Facts
- **Deductible expenses** must be incurred wholly and exclusively in producing
  income and must not be capital in nature or specifically disallowed (e.g.,
  private or non-business costs).
- **Capital allowances** are claimed on qualifying plant and machinery in place
  of accounting depreciation, which is not deductible.
- **Unutilised items** (capital allowances, trade losses, and approved
  donations) can be **carried forward** against future income, subject to the
  **shareholding test** (and, for capital allowances, the same-business test).
  Current-year losses and capital allowances may also be **carried back**
  (capped) to the immediately preceding YA.
- **Group relief** lets current-year unutilised capital allowances, trade losses,
  and donations be transferred between Singapore-incorporated members of the
  **same 75% group**.
- **CIT Rebate (year-specific):** the Budget may grant a rebate on tax payable.
  For **YA 2026** this is **50% of corporate tax payable**, with the total benefit
  (rebate plus a minimum cash grant for active companies that employed at least
  one local employee) capped at **$40,000**. Confirm the current YA's terms
  before quoting a figure.

## Summary (own words)
Chargeable income is profit after deductible expenses and capital allowances.
Where a company cannot use its deductions in a year, the unused losses, capital
allowances, and donations can move forward (or, within limits, back, or across a
75% group), provided the ownership tests are met. On top of that, the Budget
often grants a year-specific CIT rebate that reduces the final tax bill.

## Officer notes
- Separate revenue expenses (deductible) from capital outlays (capital
  allowances) before computing chargeable income.
- Check the shareholding and same-business tests before allowing carried-forward
  items, and the 75% relationship before allowing group relief.
- Treat the CIT Rebate as year-specific: verify the current YA's percentage and
  cap before quoting.
