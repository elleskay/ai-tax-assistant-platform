# Individual income tax: residency and rates

**Last verified:** 2026-06-28. Source: IRAS, "Individual Income Tax rates" and
"Tax residency of individuals" (https://www.iras.gov.sg).

## Facts
- Singapore taxes individuals on a **progressive resident-rate** scale from **0%**
  on the first $20,000 of chargeable income up to a **top marginal rate of 24%**
  (from Year of Assessment 2024) for chargeable income above $1,000,000.
- An individual is generally a **tax resident** for a Year of Assessment if they
  are a Singapore Citizen or PR who normally resides here, or a foreigner who
  stayed or worked in Singapore for **at least 183 days** in the previous year.
- **Non-residents'** employment income is taxed at the higher of a **flat 15%**
  or the resident rates; certain other income is taxed at **24%**.

## Summary (own words)
Residents are taxed on a banded scale: the first slice of chargeable income is
tax-free and each higher band is taxed at a higher rate, reaching 24% at the top.
Residency turns mainly on the 183-day rule. Non-residents are taxed differently,
usually a flat rate on employment income, so confirming residency status is the
first step in any individual case.

## Officer notes
- Chargeable income is assessed per Year of Assessment (the year after the income
  year). Always state the relevant YA in a reply.
- For split-year or short-stay foreigners, check the 183-day rule and any tax
  treaty before quoting a rate.
