# Individual income tax: common reliefs and deductions

**Last verified:** 2026-06-28. Source: IRAS, "Tax reliefs, rebates and deductions"
(https://www.iras.gov.sg).

## Facts
- **Earned Income Relief**: up to $1,000 (below 55), higher for older taxpayers
  and those with disabilities.
- **CPF relief**: relief for compulsory and voluntary CPF contributions, subject
  to CPF contribution caps.
- **SRS (Supplementary Retirement Scheme)** contribution cap: **$15,300** for
  Singapore Citizens and PRs, **$35,700** for foreigners.
- **Personal Income Tax Relief Cap**: total personal reliefs are capped at
  **$80,000** per Year of Assessment.
- Other common reliefs: Qualifying/Working Mother Child Relief, Parent Relief,
  Spouse Relief, NSman relief, course fees relief, life-insurance relief.

## Summary (own words)
Reliefs reduce chargeable income; rebates reduce tax payable. The most common
ones are earned-income, CPF, and SRS, plus a family of family-related reliefs.
Two caps matter in almost every case: the SRS contribution cap and the overall
$80,000 personal relief cap, which limits how much total relief one taxpayer can
claim in a YA.

## Officer notes
- When a taxpayer lists several reliefs, check the $80,000 cap before confirming
  the total. Excess relief above the cap is disregarded.
- SRS relief is only given for contributions within the annual cap.
